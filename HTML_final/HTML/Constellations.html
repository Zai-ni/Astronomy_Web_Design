What are constellations?
Constellations are a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern that are traditionally named after their apparent form or identified with a mythological figure. This webpage shows and explains some of the 88 officially recognized constellations. The below image consists of the constellations that are discussed. Click on a constellation for more information.



Aries

Aries is the 39th largest constellation in the night sky. Aries has just six stars making them quite dim. Aries can be seen best in the month of December in the northern hemisphere.
Taurus

Taurus the bull is the 17th largest constellation in the night sky. One of the brightest stars, Aldebaran, is located in this constellation. It could be seen between Fall and Spring in the northern hemisphere.




Gemini

Gemini is the 30th largest constellation in the night sky. The word “Gemini” means twins in Latin. It can be seen in the winter months from the northern hemisphere.




Cancer

Cancer is the dimmest of the 12 constellations in the night sky. One of the famous exoplanets, ‘Cancri 55 e’, also known as the ‘diamond world’ is located in this constellation. Cancer will be seen in early spring in the northern hemisphere.
Leo

Leo is the 12th largest of all constellations in the night sky. Leo is the most prominent constellation in the night sky. Leo the lion can be seen from anywhere except Antartica. This could be seen during the spring in the northern hemisphere.
Virgo

Virgo is the 2nd largest constellation in the night sky. Virgo means ‘the virgin’ in Latin. It is visible in the northern hemisphere during spring and summer.




Libra

Libra means “weighinhg scales” in Latin. It is best seen in the northern hemisphee in the late spring or early summer. It is the only constellation of the Zodiac to be named after an inanimate object rather than an animal or a character. It is located in the northern hemisphere in late spring / early summer.
Scorpius

Scorpius is one of the brightest constellations in the sky. The Latin word ‘Scorpius’ is translated to ‘scorpion’ in English. It could be observed close to the southern horizon in the northern hemisphere.




Sagittarius

Sagittarius is the 15ht largest constellation in the night sky. Sagittarius is the Latin name of archer. It is best viewed in August.




Capricornus

Capricornus is the 40th brightest constellation. The name ‘Capricornus’ was originated from Latin meaning the ‘goat horn’. It could be best viewed during the month of September.




Aquarius

Aquarius is the 10th largest constellation in the night sky. It is best viewed during October.
Pisces

Pisces is the 14th largest constellation in the night sky. Pisces is the Latin word for fish. Pisces can be seen in autumn in the northern hemisphere.
