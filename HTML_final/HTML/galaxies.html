Milky Way Galaxy

The Milky Way Galaxy, our celestial home, is a magnificent barred spiral galaxy that stretches across a vast expanse of space. Spanning approximately 100,000 light-years in diameter, it is estimated to contain over 200 billion stars, along with countless planets, asteroids, and other celestial objects. From our vantage point within the galaxy, we observe a luminous band of stars arching across the night sky, known as the Milky Way.

Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy, also known as Messier 31 or M31, is a breathtaking spiral galaxy located approximately 2.5 million light-years away from Earth. It holds the distinction of being the closest spiral galaxy to our own Milky Way, making it an object of great fascination and study for astronomers. Spanning a diameter of about 220,000 light-years, Andromeda is larger than our galaxy, showcasing its grandeur.

Sombrero Galaxy

The Sombrero Galaxy, also known as Messier 104 or M104, is a captivating spiral galaxy located approximately 28 million light-years away from Earth. Its distinctive appearance has earned it the nickname "Sombrero" due to its striking resemblance to a wide-brimmed Mexican hat. This galaxy stands out among the celestial wonders with its unique structure and fascinating features.

Whirlpool Galaxy

The Whirlpool Galaxy, also known as Messier 51 or M51, is a captivating spiral galaxy located approximately 23 million light-years away from Earth. It is renowned for its stunning appearance and intricate dynamics resulting from a gravitational interaction with a smaller companion galaxy.
